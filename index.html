My programing journey started when I was at 7th grade, me and my friend saw tons of videos about programmers and how much money they have - we wanted to be the same. I watched lots of videos about what language should I pick, my decision was JS. I really wanted to be full stack dev, I even convienced my parents to buy a 1 year course for crazy money (Haven't even finished it). After this course I really burned out of programming, because I thought that that all development as boring as webdev.

But I was wrong about it. After smth like half of the year I discovered python and crazy things that you can do with it. This lang was like a second breath for me, I started to learn everything that was interesting for me - telegram bots, simple scripts, some crypto integrations. But I can't say that I created at leas one good project, it's all was just something to play with. And some time of just learning, not creating I burned out second time.

And we're here now. I came back from my burnings and just creating stuff I like and learn as I do, not just watching tutorials as I did previously. Now I don't really prioritize to get job as a programmer, just create cool shi and enjoy the progress. My main study direction is engineering rn, because programming is too cooked as career path.
